Awards Desk

A guide for faculty submitting student work and administrators managing entries.

The Awards Entry Desk lets you submit student journalism work to competitions like Hearst, BEA, and NPPA. Each submission captures the project details, student information, and publication metadata.

Getting Started

Open jarradhenderson.github.io/awards-desk/ in any modern web browser. You will see the submission form with a sticky deadline countdown bar at the top.

Step 1: Select Competition & Category

Start by choosing which competition you are submitting to (Hearst, BEA, NPPA, etc.). Once you pick a competition, the Category dropdown populates with the available categories for that competition. The deadline for your selected category will display in the sticky bar at the top.

Step 2: Enter Project Details

Project Title: The name of the work being submitted (e.g., "Flint Water Crisis: A Year Later").

Date Published: When the work was originally published or broadcast (mm/dd/yyyy).

Published URL: The live link where the work appears. For multimedia, paste the Embed or Share URL. For print or broadcast work, paste a link to where it lives online (your outlet's website, portfolio, etc.). A link is always required.

Pro tip: Double-check that your Published URL is correct and starts with https://. The judges must be able to access it, or the entry may be disqualified.

Step 3: Select Course or Outlet

Choose which course or news outlet the work was produced for (JRN 410, JRN 492, WKAR, The State News, etc.). Select "Independent / Freelance" if the work was not done for a course or outlet.

Step 4: List Students Involved

Add each student who worked on the project. The first student listed is marked as primary and will appear in the awards dashboard. For each student, you must enter their full name and classification (Freshman, Sophomore, Junior, Senior).

Major selection: Check all majors that apply. If a student is undeclared or has a different major, select "Other" and type it in the text field. You can add up to 6 students per entry.

Click the green "+ Add another student" button to add more students.

Step 5: Submit

Review your entry and click the "Submit entry" button. A success screen appears with an option to email yourself a copy of the submission for your records. Your entry is now live in the awards management system.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Double-check your URL. The link must work and must be publicly accessible. If the judges cannot reach it, the entry may be disqualified.
  • Get the student info right. Verify each student's name and classification before submitting. Mistakes here can affect the final awards record.
  • Know your deadline. The countdown bar at the top shows when your competition category is due. Once the deadline passes, the form will no longer accept new entries for that category.
  • Submit early. There is no advantage to waiting. Entries are recorded by submission time, not by quality or recency.

The Awards Control dashboard is a private interface for reviewing all submitted entries, updating their status, exporting data for judging, and managing competitions and deadlines.

Accessing the Admin Dashboard

Visit jarradhenderson.github.io/awards-desk/admin.html to open the dashboard. There is no password; the system assumes only authorized personnel have this link. Keep this URL private.

Dashboard Layout

The dashboard displays a live table of all submissions. Each row shows the project title, student names, competition and category, status badge (Pending, Submitted, Winner, Honorable Mention, Rejected), course/outlet, days until deadline, and publication date.

The counter in the top right shows how many entries match your current filters.

Filtering Entries

Use the four filter dropdowns at the top to narrow the table:

  • Competition: Show only Hearst, BEA, NPPA, etc.
  • Category: Show only a specific category within a competition.
  • Academic Year: Filter by 2024-2025, 2025-2026, etc.
  • Course / Outlet: Show submissions from one course or outlet.

You can stack filters. For example, filter to "Hearst" competition + "Photography I" category + "2026-2027" year to see only that specific cohort.

Year Tabs

At the bottom of the screen, year tabs let you quickly switch between academic years. Click a year tab to filter the entire dashboard to that year. The "All years" tab shows entries across all years.

Editing Entries

Click any row in the table to open an edit panel on the right. You can update any field:

  • Status: Change from Pending to Submitted, Winner, Honorable Mention, or Rejected. Useful for tracking entries through the judging process.
  • Project Title, Competition, Category, Course: Fix typos or correct submission mistakes.
  • Dates & URLs: Update publication dates and links if the original link breaks or is updated.
  • Student Info: Correct name, classification, and major if the student info was wrong on submission.

Read-only fields like "Days Until Due" and "Submitted On" cannot be edited. Click "Save changes" to write updates to the system. Click "Cancel" to discard changes and close the drawer.

Exporting Data

Click the "Export CSV" button to download the entries currently visible on screen as a CSV file. This respects your active filters and year tab. For example, if you are viewing only Hearst Photography I entries from 2026-2027, only those entries will be in the CSV.

Use this CSV to send entry lists to judges, create competition rosters, or analyze entry trends.

Refreshing Data

Click the "Refresh" button to reload entries from the live system. New submissions appear automatically, but refresh if you want to see the latest entries without reloading the page.

Submission Failed: "Failed to fetch"

Cause: Network error or server unreachable.

Fix: Check your internet connection. Make sure the Published URL is complete and starts with https://. Try again in a few seconds. If the problem persists, contact Jarrad.

The form is slow or does not load

Cause: Browser cache or slow network.

Fix: Hard-refresh the page (Cmd+Shift+R on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows). Try a different browser or device. Clear your browser cache.

I entered a student but can't add more

Cause: You have reached the maximum of 6 students.

Fix: The form allows up to 6 students per entry. If you need to add more, create a second entry for the additional students.

The admin dashboard shows no entries

Cause: Either no entries have been submitted yet, or all entries are filtered out.

Fix: Click "Refresh" to reload from the live system. Check that your filters are set to "All". Click the "All years" tab at the bottom. If there are truly no entries, wait for submissions to come in.

I cannot find an entry I just submitted

Cause: The dashboard has not refreshed yet, or the entry did not save.

Fix: Click "Refresh" in the dashboard. If the entry still does not appear, ask the faculty member to check their submission confirmation email. If they received a confirmation, the entry was saved and will appear after a refresh.

An edited entry did not save

Cause: Network error during the save.

Fix: Try editing the entry again. If a message appears saying "Save failed", wait a few seconds and try again. If the problem persists, refresh the page and re-open the entry to see if your changes were saved.

CSV export is empty or incomplete

Cause: Your filters are too restrictive, or you have no entries matching the filters.

Fix: Check your active filters at the top and your year tab at the bottom. Make sure you are not accidentally filtering out all entries. Click "All" on each filter to reset them.
